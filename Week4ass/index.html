Minimum Wages Around the World
There is always the question of whether it is better to have a nation where everyone is poor or a nation where some are rich and most are poor. Eradicating poverty is an elusive pursuit, but nevertheless, it is an important one. The imposition of minimum wages is one of the potential measures taken to lower poverty rates.

Hover over each country to learn what the current minimum wage rates are in 2020.

How do we interpret this data? Does a lower minimum wage rate lead to a lower poverty rate? The answer of course, is that, it depends.

T.H. Gindling, researcher of the World of Labor, claims that whether or not minimum wages reduces poverty depends not only on whether formal sector workers lose jobs as a result, but also on whether low-wage workers live in poor households, how widely minimum wages are enforced, how minimum wages affect informal workers, and whether social safety nets are in place.
